Conflict Resolution

Discuss a time you had an emotional conflict with someone. What was it about? What happened?

One of the most notable times I've had an emotional conflict with someone has been over the future direction of the startup I helped start.

What was the source of the conflict?

We were both at odds regarding which healthcare market we should traget, and she and I had different opinions regarding what to do.

How did you handle that conflict?

The resolution of the conflict was straight forward - we ended up running empirical tests and doing informatoinal interviews into different markets before making a choice that we both agreed on.

Did your actions make the conflict better or worse?

My actions in suggesting we run a few experiments helped solve things for the better - it helped both objectively test different ideas and helped us pick the best choice for us.

If you could go back, what would you do differently, if anything? What did you learn from this experience?

I've reflected on this and other circumstances of disagreement in the past, and I've come to the conclusion that that particular example of disagreement is a special one with a definitive decision. I think the core of defusing conflict is first understand another person's position and then seek to be understood. Afterwards, trying to find objective ways of appealing to people both logically and emotionally is important.
